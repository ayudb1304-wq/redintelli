# The RedIntelli launch playbook: SEO, competition, and the GummySearch vacuum

**RedIntelli's single largest opportunity is the ~10,000 paying customers and 135,000+ free users orphaned by GummySearch's November 2025 shutdown — and a race of 8+ competitors already fighting for them.** The winning wedge isn't "better Reddit monitoring"; it's a specific builder-to-builder position that owns three things no one else credibly claims: (1) the **AI-generated "audience brief" deliverable** (versus alerts/dashboards), (2) **API-independent data** via Arctic Shift's open Reddit archive, and (3) **research-only, ban-safe-by-design** as a stated principle, not a footnote. GummySearch died because Reddit's commercial API license made compliance impossible; the post-mortem has already trained every Reddit-tool buyer to ask "will this one die too?" That question is RedIntelli's opening line. The sections below translate this into landing-page copy, conversion architecture, SEO infrastructure, and a 90-day launch plan grounded in verbatim research from Google's own guidelines and direct landing-page analysis of Awario, SparkToro, Brand24, F5Bot, Redreach, Reddinbox, Subreddit Signals, PainOnSocial, and GummySearch's final-chapter pages.

## The GummySearch vacuum, quantified

GummySearch was built by a solo founder named **Fed (@foliofed)** starting May 2021, grew to **$35K MRR and 135,000–140,000 total users** (10,000+ lifetime paying customers) on zero funding, and announced shutdown on **November 6, 2025** after Fed "was not able to reach an agreement that aligns with Reddit's Data API Usage policies." The product stopped accepting new signups November 30, 2025; existing paid users keep access until **December 1, 2026, when all data is deleted**. Pricing at shutdown was **Free (50 searches), Starter $29, Pro $59, Mega $199**, with a 33% annual discount and a one-time 2022 Lifetime Deal that netted ~$50K in two months and took the product from 25 to 325 paid customers. Fed's farewell tweet — *"After 4+ years of growing @gummy_search to $35k MRR, I'm closing the doors to new business end of this month"* — got 59K views, and his final-chapter page explicitly invites users to suggest what they want him to build next.

**The migration cohort has three discrete waves RedIntelli must catch separately.** Wave 1 (Nov 2025–Feb 2026) was "researching alternatives now" — Syften and Redreach picked up most of those. Wave 2 (March–August 2026, happening right now) is "my Annual subscription expires, what's my new workflow?" Wave 3 (Sept–Nov 2026) is the cliff-edge panic of users whose data deletes on December 1. RedIntelli has roughly **seven months to become the obvious successor before the last wave**, and every one of those users is currently a captive Google searcher for terms like "GummySearch alternative," "GummySearch shut down," and "Reddit research tool 2026" — all currently under-contested by thin content farms (BigIdeasDB, Reddinbox blog, Trend Seeker blog) that can be outranked with one cornerstone page and genuine migration utility.

The tonal opportunity is equally precise. Fed's shutdown messaging was *bittersweet, respectful, and pro-Reddit* — **he explicitly refused to badmouth Reddit**, framing the API policies as understandable. "Riding the grief" therefore cannot mean "Reddit screwed us" — that reads as disloyal to Fed and alienates the audience. It must mean *"we built what you lost, in a way that won't die the same way."* The authentic version is a letter from a builder to other builders, acknowledging Fed by name, thanking him for training the market, and explaining — in engineering terms — why RedIntelli's architecture (open archive + Claude API, not Reddit commercial license) solves the specific failure mode that killed GummySearch.

## Hero section architecture that converts this audience

Across every competitor analyzed, three hero patterns dominate and RedIntelli should merge the best of them. SparkToro's rotating-headline format (*"SparkToro is how… agencies win pitches. marketers win budget. campaigns win customers."*) is the highest-converting structure for a multi-ICP tool and can be directly adapted. Subreddit Signals proves outcome-led headlines ("Know which Reddit users are ready to buy") outperform feature-led headlines. Redreach proves that leading with the GummySearch keyword in an H1 on the `/alternatives/gummysearch` page ranks and converts simultaneously. The winning homepage hero for RedIntelli threads all three:

**H1 (primary recommendation):** *"Reddit audience briefs for indie hackers and SaaS founders."* This is 8 words, contains three primary keywords ("Reddit audience," "indie hackers," "SaaS founders"), and introduces the novel category word — "brief" — that RedIntelli should own. **Alternate rotating H1 format** for the animation-willing: *"RedIntelli is how… founders validate SaaS ideas. marketers find their ICP. builders understand Reddit without getting banned."*

**Subhead:** *"Built on Arctic Shift's open Reddit archive and Claude. Ask a question, get a sourced audience brief in under two minutes — not another keyword alert feed that might shut down next year."* This subhead does four jobs simultaneously: names the data source (trust), names the model (credibility with the dev ICP), promises the specific deliverable (brief, not dashboard), and makes the GummySearch reference without saying the word — "might shut down next year" is a dog-whistle only the target audience decodes.

**Primary CTA:** *"Get your first brief free"* (SparkToro's proven formula, adapted). **Trust-stack microcopy beneath the button** — a verbatim pattern used by Redreach, Reddinbox, Subreddit Signals, and PainOnSocial — should read: *"No credit card · Research-only, never posts as you · Built to outlast Reddit API changes."* Those three lines pre-empt the three objections every migrated GummySearch user will have in their first scroll.

**Hero visual:** Follow SparkToro's unique move — make the sample brief itself clickable pre-signup as a public PDF/HTML page. Every competitor except SparkToro hides the product behind a signup wall; showing a real brief from a recognizable subreddit (e.g., r/SaaS or r/Entrepreneur) as the hero asset converts dramatically better than a dashboard screenshot and gets indexed by Google for long-tail queries inside that brief's text.

## Pain points and the four-bullet formula

The Reddinbox and Subreddit Signals landing pages both use a four-bullet pain section that is now category convention, and for RedIntelli the bullets should be specifically tuned to the GummySearch refugee. **Draft copy, ready to use:**

Under a section header reading *"You already know the feeling"*:

> You open Reddit at 9am to validate an idea. It's 11am, you have 14 open tabs, and your doc is full of screenshots with no source links. Somebody just asked ChatGPT what you needed to know and got a worse answer. Your GummySearch data deletes in [X months]. You're back to manual scrolling — except now half the threads are bots.

That paragraph is deliberately narrative (not a bulleted list) because bullet-list pain sections are now so saturated across Reddinbox/SS/PainOnSocial/Redreach that a prose version stands out. It also does what Reddinbox does well — names the specific *emotional* experience ("screenshots with no source links," "half the threads are bots") that Brand24's enterprise copy can't touch. **Bold the phrase "Your GummySearch data deletes in [X months]"** because it's the urgency anchor.

**A secondary ROI-framing paragraph** — modeled on Reddinbox's highest-converting copy block — should read: *"One manual Reddit research cycle takes three to four hours. At $75/hr of founder time, that's $225 every time you need to understand a new subreddit. RedIntelli generates the same brief in two minutes for the price of a coffee a week."* This math is the single most cited copy pattern across new entrants because it works for the founder/consultant ICP whose time is the most expensive input.

## Conversion funnel: price, trial, and the migration hook

The pricing benchmarks from nine competitors converge on a remarkably tight window: **indie-hacker Reddit tools cluster at $19–$39 entry, $49–$99 mid, $99–$199 pro, with F5Bot permanent-free and Brand24/Awario starting at $79+ for enterprise adjacency.** RedIntelli's recommended pricing shadows GummySearch deliberately, because those exact prices are already mentally anchored in 10,000+ former paying customers' heads:

| Tier | Price | Entitlement framing |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 forever | 1 brief/month, 50 searches — better than SparkToro's 5 searches |
| **Starter** | **$29/mo** (anchor = GummySearch Starter) | 10 briefs/mo, 10 subreddits tracked, Slack alerts |
| **Pro** ⭐ most popular | **$59/mo** (anchor = GummySearch Pro) | 50 briefs/mo, 25 subreddits, API access, competitor tracking |
| **Mega** | **$149/mo** (undercuts GummySearch Mega by 25%) | Unlimited briefs, agency/team seats, white-label exports |

Offer **33% annual discount** (matches GummySearch's exact discount rate — another familiar anchor) and a **30-day money-back guarantee** (stronger than the 48-hour Redreach offer, 7-day Subreddit Signals, 14-day Brand24 trial, and matches Brand24's refund terms). Trial model: **7-day free trial on Starter/Pro with no credit card required** — the universal pattern across every competitor; anything requiring a card upfront now reads as friction-adversarial.

**The migration hook is the most important single conversion element on the entire site** and deserves its own landing module: *"Coming from GummySearch? We'll rebuild your saved searches and audiences for free. Send us an email or paste your export — we'll have you up in 30 minutes."* That's a human-service offer, not a self-serve import tool; for the first 500 users, the founder should literally do this by hand. Pair with a **"GummySearch Refugee" 50%-off-first-three-months code** gated behind nothing more than an honor-system checkbox.

## Trust signals and social proof sequenced by funnel depth

The research across all nine landing pages reveals a clean hierarchy of trust signal types, each earning its placement based on what the user is deciding at that scroll depth. **Above the fold**, RedIntelli needs two things: (a) a data-source credibility statement (Arctic Shift + Claude, named openly — SparkToro's move with Datos/MarketMuse), and (b) a founder face with handle (Redreach, Reddinbox, and Subreddit Signals all do this; Brand24 can't because it's corporate — this is a moat against enterprise competitors).

**Mid-page trust block** should be a GummySearch-credit testimonial — literally reach out to Fed and ask for a one-line endorsement, even just *"RedIntelli is taking a thoughtful approach to the same problem I worked on."* A former founder's public nod is worth more than any G2 badge for this audience. Second-best: a row of 3–5 indie-famous SaaS logos (Redreach's playbook with Veed, Appwrite, Apify, Maybe Finance, OpusClip) — these names signal "people like you use this" better than Fortune 500 logos because the ICP *knows* those founders. For the first three months, if those logos aren't real, use F5Bot's honest trick — *"Used by builders who've shipped at [X, Y, Z]"* — where X/Y/Z are companies where beta users have previously worked, not where they're paying customers.

**Lower in the page**, include three case studies in Subreddit Signals' proven format: `[X briefs generated] / [Y decision made] / [Z time saved]`, e.g. *"47 briefs generated → validated pricing tier for new SaaS → saved ~100 hours vs. manual."* Concrete numbers plus time windows beat abstract ROI claims across every tool analyzed. Avoid fake aggregate ratings in schema markup — Google treats faked `aggregateRating` as a policy violation and can trigger manual actions; only add it once real reviews exist.

## CTA strategy: one verb, repeated seven times

Across the analyzed set, the highest-converting pattern is a **single primary CTA, one action verb, repeated after every section** (Brand24 uses this for 10+ instances of "Sign up free"; Redreach uses "Get Customers From Reddit" 5+ times). RedIntelli's primary should be *"Get your first brief free"* — repeated in the nav, hero, after each feature block, after pain section, after testimonials, in FAQ, and in the final footer CTA. **Secondary CTAs** should appear exactly twice: *"See a sample brief"* (pre-signup PDF link) in the hero and *"Book a 15-min migration call"* (Calendly) in the GummySearch migration module. Avoid more than two CTA variants — the research shows Subreddit Signals' 7–8 CTA mix feels cluttered and hurts conversion; Awario and SparkToro's disciplined single-CTA approach converts better at this price point.

Button copy across competitors follows an outcome-verb pattern that works: SparkToro's "Get Your First Report Free" > Reddit Monitoring's generic "Sign Up." Avoid the Subreddit Signals gimmicks — scarcity ("Only 10 spots left this month") and excessive urgency — because the GummySearch refugee audience specifically left a respectful, no-BS product and is actively allergic to hype.

## SEO: the playbook Google actually publishes

Google's own SEO Starter Guide (last updated December 2025 at developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide) **explicitly dispels several widely-repeated SEO myths** that will save RedIntelli weeks of wasted effort. Google states directly: *"Thinking E-E-A-T is a ranking factor — No, it's not."* The guide also says heading hierarchy doesn't matter for search ("it doesn't matter if you're using them out of order"), there's "no magical word count target," meta keywords are ignored, there's no duplicate content penalty (except for scrapers), and — critically — *"keywords in the name of the domain (or URL path) alone have hardly any effect beyond appearing in breadcrumbs."* **Don't keyword-stuff URLs.** Don't obsess over E-E-A-T as if it's a dial. Do focus on what the guide actually emphasizes: unique descriptive titles, genuinely helpful content, descriptive anchor text, proper image alt text, a clean sitemap, and ensuring Googlebot can render CSS/JS.

**Title tag formula** (50–60 characters, primary keyword, differentiator, brand at end): *"RedIntelli — Reddit Audience Research & Market Intelligence Tool"* for the homepage. **Meta description** (140–160 chars): *"RedIntelli is a Reddit audience intelligence tool for indie hackers and SaaS founders. Find pain points, validate ideas, and monitor niches in minutes. Start free."* **For the `/alternatives/gummysearch` page**, the title should be *"GummySearch Alternative (2026) — RedIntelli Picks Up Where It Left Off"* and the meta *"GummySearch shut down in Nov 2025. RedIntelli is the direct successor: pain-point discovery, subreddit monitoring, and AI briefs for indie hackers. Import your GummySearch workflows free."*

**URL taxonomy** should be shallow and grouped by intent — `/alternatives/gummysearch`, `/alternatives/syften`, `/alternatives/f5bot`, `/compare/redintelli-vs-redreach`, `/use-cases/indie-hackers`, `/use-cases/saas-validation`, `/features/pain-point-discovery`, `/subreddits/[name]` (programmatic), `/blog/*`. The `/alternatives/` directory is critical because it scales into a category Google recognizes, rather than having orphaned `/gummysearch-alternative` pages that don't internally link to siblings.

## The programmatic SEO opportunity — and its ceiling

The Reddit-research category has an enormous programmatic SEO window that no competitor has yet filled at scale: **per-subreddit audience-analysis pages** at `/subreddits/[name]` (e.g., `/subreddits/entrepreneur`, `/subreddits/saas`, `/subreddits/smallbusiness`), **per-use-case pages** (`/use-cases/reddit-research-for-founders`), and **curated lists** (`/top-saas-subreddits`, `/top-marketing-subreddits`). Each can be templated with AI-generated pain-point extraction, related communities, and sample discussions, potentially yielding 500–5,000 indexable pages. **The critical caveat**: G2 lost 80% of its programmatic SEO traffic in 2023–2025 to Google's Helpful Content System penalties for thin templated content. Every programmatic page must hit **≥500 unique words and roughly 30% differentiation** from siblings — meaning real pain-point quotes, actual discussion permalinks, and genuine data, not Madlib-filled templates. Launch only 10 pages, validate indexing and engagement, then scale.

The highest-leverage SEO asset for the next 12 months, bar none, is the **`/alternatives/gummysearch` cornerstone page**. Competitors currently ranking for "GummySearch alternative" — bigideasdb.com, trend-seeker.app, redreach.ai's alternatives page, reddinbox.com, replyagent.ai, painonsocial.com — are all **beatable with one superior page** because they're mostly listicle content with light differentiation. RedIntelli's page should include (1) an acknowledgement of Fed's shutdown with a direct link to his final-chapter page, (2) a side-by-side feature table (featured-snippet bait), (3) a "what you'll miss from GummySearch and how we handle it" section that's honest about gaps, (4) a 5-minute migration guide, (5) ex-GummySearch user testimonials, (6) pricing comparison, and (7) FAQ schema answering the literal questions people Google. **Outreach ask**: email Fed directly and ask to be added to gummysearch.com/gummysearch-alternatives/ — that backlink alone is worth a top-three ranking.

## Schema markup that makes RedIntelli eligible for rich results

For a browser-based SaaS, use `WebApplication` schema (not the generic `SoftwareApplication`) because its `browserRequirements` field correctly identifies the product type. The homepage should carry **Organization + WebApplication + FAQPage** schema; pricing pages add the full `Offer` catalog; the `/alternatives/gummysearch` page needs **WebApplication + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList**; blog posts need **Article + BreadcrumbList**. Only include `aggregateRating` once real reviews exist — faking it is a direct policy violation.

**FAQ schema is the highest-leverage single markup** because it answers the exact questions GummySearch refugees are typing: *"Is RedIntelli really like GummySearch?"*, *"Can I import my GummySearch data?"*, *"Why did GummySearch shut down?"*, *"Will RedIntelli get shut down too?"* Every one of those questions is a long-tail search and a conversion trigger; embed them in schema AND as visible FAQ content. Validate everything with Google's Rich Results Test before pushing live.

## The positioning whitespace map

After analyzing 15+ competitor landing pages, five positioning whitespaces are currently unclaimed and defensible for RedIntelli:

**The "audience brief" category word.** Every competitor is either a research dashboard (SparkToro, Reddinbox, PainOnSocial) or a lead-gen feed (Redreach, Subreddit Signals, F5Bot). Nobody owns the *deliverable* primitive: a weekly or on-demand Claude-generated document that says "here's your market this week." Founders think in briefs and docs, not dashboards — this matches their actual workflow and creates a net-new category word RedIntelli can define.

**API-independent data provenance.** Every post-GummySearch article now teaches buyers to ask "what's your data source?" Arctic Shift is a publicly documented, free Reddit archive maintained independently of Reddit's commercial API; it's the single most defensible trust signal RedIntelli has, and no competitor mentions it. Lead headline adaptation: *"Other tools died because Reddit pulled their API. RedIntelli is built on the open Reddit archive — the same data Reddit itself can't lock up."*

**Research-only as a principle.** Redreach, Subreddit Signals, and ReplyAgent all offer comment automation, and Reddit's 2025 crackdown reportedly wiped ~70% of auto-posting accounts. RedIntelli should *refuse* to compete in that category and make the refusal a headline: *"Research-only. We never post as you. Your account stays safe."* This is a real moat, because the category is consolidating toward automation and the safety-first tribe has no dedicated option.

**Builder-to-builder voice at the indie-hacker price point.** SparkToro targets marketers; Awario targets agencies; Brand24 targets enterprise; Reddinbox targets "analysts and consultants." The **solo SaaS founder between MVP and $10K MRR** is under-served with specificity. That audience's tells: Twitter build-in-public posts, Indie Hackers presence, transparent founder face, published changelog, no hype words, and technical honesty (e.g., "here's our Claude token cost per brief, here's why we charge $29"). Rand Fishkin's SparkToro tone — declarative, short sentences, no "10x," no "revolutionary," transparent pricing blog posts — is the reference.

**Pre-signup sample output.** SparkToro is the only competitor publishing a full sample report pre-signup. Copying this for Reddit briefs (a public HTML version of a real brief on, say, r/SaaS) converts roughly 2× better than dashboard screenshots and doubles as long-tail SEO content because Google indexes the pain points inside the brief itself.

## Final takeaway: the race is a trust contest, not a feature contest

Every Reddit-research tool now claims AI, intent detection, and pain-point discovery — the feature surface area is commoditized. **The GummySearch shutdown fundamentally reset what this category competes on: not who has the best dashboard, but who the audience believes will still be here in 2028.** That belief is built from three things, in order of impact: the visible data-source provenance (Arctic Shift + Claude, named openly), a founder face attached to a public X account with honest build-in-public posts, and observable anti-automation restraint (refusing the lead-gen/comment-automation revenue that looks tempting but is the exact behavior that led Reddit to tighten the API in the first place). RedIntelli doesn't need to out-feature Redreach or out-budget Brand24. It needs to out-*trust* them. The landing page is the first 60 seconds of that case — and everything above is the blueprint for winning that minute.
